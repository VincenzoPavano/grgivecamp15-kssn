What is a community school?

A community school is a place and a set of partnerships. KSSN community schools bring physical health, behavioral health, and social services directly into the school building through partnerships with state and local agencies:

  • A Community School Coordinator coordinates basic-needs resources for families and develops partnerships with nonprofits, faith-based organizations, and businesses
  • A Site Team Clinician provides behavioral health and prevention services for children, youth, and families
  • A DHS Worker provides assistance to strengthen families and improve financial stability, and monitors attendance
  • A Nurse or Health Aide provides physical health needs

KSSN is a community school initiative serving 18,000 students in 30 schools across nine districts in Kent County
